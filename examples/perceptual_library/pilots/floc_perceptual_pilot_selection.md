# FLOC* Perceptual Pilot Selection

Generated: 2026-05-15
Mode: controlled, reversible, non-runtime
Source: public FLOC* case-study corpus

## Purpose

This pilot set starts converting the public FLOC* corpus into operational perceptual structure without connecting it to scoring, prompts, reports, Visual Signature runtime, or ingestion.

The cases are selected for contrast, not quality. The goal is to pressure-test whether the `perceptual_case_record` schema can hold different visual languages without collapsing into generic abstraction.

## Selected Pilots

| Case | Role | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Charms | editorial / minimal / atmospheric | Soft editorial surface, restrained identity cues, emotional and ritual language, prior local visual observation artifacts available. |
| D4DATA | experimental / motion-heavy | Kendo metaphor, datamosh transitions, MetaHuman production, explicit motion and audiovisual language. |
| Grandvalira Resorts | corporate / system | Multi-brand design system, component library, foundations, scalability, cohesion across resort identities. |

## Why These Three

Charms tests whether Brand3 can describe softness, silence, ritual, and emotional atmosphere without overclaiming strategy.

D4DATA tests whether Brand3 can separate metaphor, motion, and product/interface claims. It should expose whether the schema can hold audiovisual evidence without turning everything into "energy" or "innovation".

Grandvalira Resorts tests whether Brand3 can handle institutional system language: components, foundations, multi-brand cohesion, and controlled difference.

## Pilot Rules

- Do not score these records.
- Do not infer quality.
- Do not generalize case-specific metaphors into universal vocabulary.
- Do not treat FLOC* case copy as proof of visible execution unless independently anchored.
- Keep public case language separate from Brand3 interpretation.
- Mark unsupported or weakly supported inferences explicitly.
- Require human review for inferred strategic intention.
