# Editorial Readiness Policy

## 1. Purpose

Brand3 scoring and Brand3 editorial output are separate concerns.

A score can be calculated from partial, fallback-based, or uneven data. An editorial report, however, makes claims that a founder, CMO, or brand team may treat as a strategic reading. That requires a stricter standard than “a number exists.”

This policy defines how Brand3 should speak depending on report readiness. Its goal is to keep Brand3 useful without overstating what the system knows.

Core principle:

> Brand3 reports what it knows. Where it does not know, it says so and explains why.

This policy does not replace scoring. It governs the language Brand3 should use when turning scores and evidence into public-facing interpretation.

## 2. Readiness Modes

### `publishable_brand_report`

Meaning: Brand3 has enough supported material in the core dimensions to produce an editorial brand report.

Brand3 may say:

- Clear observations grounded in evidence.
- Strategic implications when they are conditional and derived from observed evidence.
- Cross-dimension tensions when both sides of the tension are supported.
- Typical decision spaces, framed as options rather than prescriptions.

Brand3 should avoid saying:

- That an outcome is happening unless evidence directly supports it.
- That a business metric changed unless measured data exists.
- That the brand “must” take one specific action.

Recommended tone:

- Editorial, specific, confident where evidence is strong.
- Clear about limits where a finding depends on owned sources or partial public data.

Example phrases:

- “The public surface consistently presents the brand as...”
- “This creates a likely tension between...”
- “A typical decision space here is...”
- “Which path matters most depends on internal priorities not visible from public data.”

Forbidden phrase patterns:

- “This is costing the brand...”
- “This reduces conversion...”
- “The brand is losing trust...”
- “The company should immediately...”
- “The correct move is...”

### `technical_diagnostic`

Meaning: Brand3 has enough data to inspect the run, but not enough supported editorial material for a full brand report.

Brand3 may say:

- What was collected.
- Which dimensions are limited.
- Which signals are missing, weak, fallback-based, or not supported.
- Why the run is not ready for editorial interpretation.

Brand3 should avoid saying:

- Strategic implications as if they were supported findings.
- Cross-dimension conclusions that depend on weak or unavailable dimensions.
- Strong qualitative labels that sound like final judgment.

Recommended tone:

- Operational, diagnostic, restrained.
- Useful for deciding whether to rerun, deepen collection, or inspect evidence.

Example phrases:

- “This run is diagnostic, not publishable.”
- “Presence has traceable support, but Perception lacks external evidence.”
- “The score exists, but the dimension is not editorial-ready because...”
- “Recommended next step: collect direct external mentions before generating findings.”

Forbidden phrase patterns:

- “The brand’s market perception is weak” when Perception is not evaluable.
- “The brand has low vitality” when Vitality is based on fallback or off-entity material.
- “This proves...” unless the supporting evidence is direct and relevant.

### `insufficient_evidence`

Meaning: Brand3 does not have enough relevant evidence to responsibly produce a report.

Brand3 may say:

- That evidence is insufficient.
- Which dimensions failed readiness.
- Which inputs were missing or rejected.
- Whether the issue is coverage, confidence, fallback data, or entity relevance.

Brand3 should avoid saying:

- Any strategic reading of the brand.
- Any aggregate editorial judgment.
- Any recommendation beyond evidence collection or rerun guidance.

Recommended tone:

- Plain, honest, non-defensive.
- The value is in explaining why Brand3 is withholding interpretation.

Example phrases:

- “Insufficient evidence to produce an editorial brand report.”
- “Two core dimensions were not evaluable from available public evidence.”
- “External material was rejected because it appears to refer to another entity.”
- “A deeper collection profile is required before Brand3 can issue a reading.”

Forbidden phrase patterns:

- “Despite limited evidence, the brand appears...”
- “The likely strategic issue is...”
- “The brand should...”
- “Overall, this is a weak brand.”

## 3. Dimension States

### `ready`

Meaning: The dimension has enough relevant, traceable evidence and sufficient confidence for editorial interpretation.

Finding phrasing:

- Use observation + evidence + implication + typical decision.
- Observation should be factual and grounded in evidence.
- Implication may be strategic, but must use conditional or probabilistic language.
- Typical decision should be plural, not a single prescription.

Strategic implications: allowed.

Recommendations: allowed only as decision spaces, not direct instructions.

How to present the dimension:

- As a supported signal.
- It can be a strength, weakness, or tension depending on evidence.

Example:

- “ATH21 consistently frames itself as a legal partner for fintech and digital assets. This may help category fit with crypto-native clients, but it also narrows the audience that immediately understands the firm. Typical decisions include clarifying the category entry point, adding proof points, or testing whether the language travels outside owned channels.”

### `observation_only`

Meaning: The dimension has relevant material, but not enough support for full strategic interpretation.

Finding phrasing:

- State only what is observable.
- Avoid implication and typical decision unless explicitly marked as withheld.
- Make the limitation visible.

Strategic implications: not allowed.

Recommendations: not allowed.

How to present the dimension:

- As a limited observation, not as a weakness.
- The limitation belongs to evidence quality, not necessarily to the brand.

Example:

- “The website presents a clear fintech/legal positioning. Implication withheld: available evidence is mostly owned-channel and does not yet show whether third parties repeat this framing.”

### `technical_only`

Meaning: The dimension has score or feature data, but the material is fallback-based, structurally weak, or not suitable for editorial output.

Finding phrasing:

- Explain the technical limitation.
- Avoid brand judgment.
- Prefer collector/readiness language over strategic language.

Strategic implications: not allowed.

Recommendations: limited to data collection or rerun actions.

How to present the dimension:

- As a diagnostic limitation.
- Do not frame it as brand weakness.

Example:

- “Coherence has a score, but the supporting evidence is fallback-based. Editorial synthesis should be withheld until direct owned-channel material is available.”

### `not_evaluable`

Meaning: The dimension lacks enough relevant evidence to evaluate.

Finding phrasing:

- Say the dimension is not evaluable.
- Explain why in concrete terms.
- Quantify rejected or missing evidence when possible.

Strategic implications: not allowed.

Recommendations: only evidence-gathering guidance.

How to present the dimension:

- As unavailable signal, not as weakness.
- Never convert “not evaluable” into “low performance.”

Example:

- “Vitality is not evaluable from relevant public evidence. Traceable sources were rejected because they refer to a different entity, not the analysed brand.”

## 4. Evidence Language

### Direct evidence

Definition: Traceable, relevant evidence that supports the claim.

Preferred language:

- “Direct evidence shows...”
- “The owned site states...”
- “The cited source describes...”

Avoid:

- Calling URL presence “direct” if entity relevance is unknown.
- Treating unrelated but traceable sources as support.

### Indirect evidence

Definition: Relevant evidence that supports an inference but does not prove it directly.

Preferred language:

- “This suggests...”
- “This is consistent with...”
- “A reasonable reading is...”

Avoid:

- “This proves...”
- “This confirms...”

### Weak evidence

Definition: Evidence with low confidence, low coverage, weak traceability, or heavy heuristic dependence.

Preferred language:

- “Weak signal...”
- “Preliminary reading...”
- “This should be treated as directional.”

Avoid:

- Strong conclusions.
- Business impact claims.
- Recommendations beyond “collect more evidence.”

### Off-entity evidence

Definition: Traceable material that appears to refer to a different entity.

Preferred language:

- “Rejected as off-entity.”
- “The public index appears to conflate the analysed brand with...”
- “This may indicate a naming collision, but it should not count toward scoring or sentiment.”

Avoid:

- Using off-entity sentiment as brand sentiment.
- Treating off-entity activity as vitality.
- Writing findings as if the other entity is related to the brand.

### Analysis notes

Definition: Model or analyzer conclusions that are not themselves source evidence.

Preferred language:

- “Analyzer note...”
- “Derived observation...”
- “Not counted as direct evidence.”

Avoid:

- Presenting analysis notes as source-backed facts.
- Counting them as direct evidence.

### Fallback values

Definition: Scores or feature values produced when primary evidence is unavailable.

Preferred language:

- “Fallback value detected.”
- “Score exists, but editorial readiness is reduced.”
- “This should not be read as average performance.”

Avoid:

- “The brand is average.”
- “The dimension is mixed” when the value is a neutral fallback.

## 5. Practical Examples

### Strong language

Use when state is `ready`.

Good:

- “Charms AI has a differentiated concept of an ‘AI Character Economy,’ but that positioning is undermined by repeated name collisions with unrelated AI products.”

Why it works:

- It names a supported strength.
- It names a supported conflict.
- It does not invent business impact.

Bad:

- “Charms AI is losing SEO traffic because of name collisions.”

Why it fails:

- Traffic loss is not measured.

### Cautious language

Use when state is `observation_only`.

Good:

- “The brand uses clear fintech/legal language on its own site. External repetition of that positioning was not sufficiently supported in this run.”

Bad:

- “The brand has strong positioning in the market.”

Why it fails:

- “In the market” requires external validation.

### Diagnostic language

Use when state is `technical_only` or mode is `technical_diagnostic`.

Good:

- “Presence has a score, but missing high-weight features make the dimension unsuitable for editorial synthesis.”

Bad:

- “Presence is weak.”

Why it fails:

- The limitation is measurement quality, not necessarily brand performance.

### Unavailable signal

Use when state is `not_evaluable`.

Good:

- “Perception is not evaluable from available public evidence. No supported external mentions were available after filtering.”

Bad:

- “Perception is low.”

Why it fails:

- Absence of usable evidence is not negative perception.

## 6. Non-goals

This policy does not change:

- Scores.
- Dimension weights.
- Feature extractors.
- Collectors.
- Prompts.
- Narrative generation yet.
- Public report rendering yet.
- Database schema.
- Historical report outputs.

It is a language and product policy. Implementation can happen later and should be phased.

## 7. Future Implementation

This policy can later inform:

- Narrative generation prompts that choose language by readiness state.
- Report warnings that distinguish brand weakness from evidence limitation.
- Diagnostic views for internal QA.
- Public report gating.
- Finding templates that explicitly separate observation, evidence, implication, and typical decision.
- Evidence summaries that distinguish traceability from entity relevance.
- Test fixtures that prevent unsupported editorial claims from reappearing.

Recommended implementation sequence:

1. Keep readiness calculation separate from scoring.
2. Add diagnostic-only visibility for readiness.
3. Use readiness to constrain narrative generation.
4. Add entity relevance before redefining “direct evidence” publicly.
5. Only then consider changing public report modes or withholding reports.
